# Overlap and Review Quality

## Primary-Study Overlap

Build a matrix:

```text
primary study x review
```

Track:

- number of primary studies per review;
- shared primary studies across reviews;
- unique primary studies;
- whether reviews include updated or duplicate evidence;
- whether review conclusions are driven by the same few primary studies.

## Quality Questions

Ask:

- Was the protocol registered?
- Were search strategies complete?
- Were two reviewers used for screening/extraction?
- Were exclusion reasons logged?
- Was risk of bias assessed?
- Were methods appropriate for synthesis?
- Was publication bias considered?
- Are conclusions proportional to evidence quality?

## Statistical Pooling Decision

Second-order pooling may be reasonable only when:

- review-level effects estimate comparable constructs;
- primary-study overlap is quantified;
- dependence is handled or sensitivity checked;
- quality differences are transparent.

If these conditions fail, use narrative umbrella synthesis or reconstruct a primary-study-level dataset.
