# Environmental and Life-Science Evidence Synthesis

## Frameworks

Use PECO for observational exposure questions:

- Population
- Exposure
- Comparator
- Outcome

Use PICO for interventions:

- Population
- Intervention
- Comparator
- Outcome

## Common Heterogeneity Sources

Environmental:

- geography;
- climate zone;
- exposure intensity;
- exposure duration;
- pollutant mixture;
- seasonality;
- land use;
- measurement instrument.

Ecological:

- species;
- trophic level;
- habitat;
- life stage;
- biodiversity metric;
- spatial scale;
- temporal scale.

Biomedical/life science:

- organism;
- tissue;
- cell type;
- assay platform;
- dose;
- treatment duration;
- disease stage;
- sex/age;
- batch/platform effects.

## Outcome Families

Examples:

- biodiversity richness or abundance;
- physiological markers;
- gene expression;
- inflammatory biomarkers;
- mortality or morbidity;
- pollutant concentration;
- emissions intensity;
- ecosystem function.

Keep outcome families separate unless a defensible effect-size transformation exists.
